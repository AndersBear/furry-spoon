  Nobody disputed that these men were the toughest of all men. As a group, they could plow over anyone who dared not worship them, even the cops. These were the big toughies, "industrial strength everything." They had the biggest trucks, motorcycles and boats. When they came around, people scattered. Their women had to give them just the right amount of emotion without ever blowing the lid off the gang. Bodies and faces were the weapons of these toughies toward any stranger who dared look at them the wrong way. Alcohol and pot were petty to this group. They needed something more powerful. Cocaine was their big deal. After all, if there were fights among themselves, coke was a required adversary. As with any other drug, surrounding peer pressure was expected. Meth-heads were tangent sub-friends of the group. Then they had this one friend who seemed to have bypassed some of the clan's ways of thinking. He was intelligent in ways that confused most people. He was more emotional than some of the women, which obviously bothered some of the big studs who were always looking for some pivot point in the conversations. Most of the men felt secretly betrayed by this guy. He hadn't paid his dues to them. He hadn't bulked up like they had. He hadn't cut his hair shorter like they had. His work wasn't as blue-collar as their work. He listened to rock music, not metal, not country. He did more blow than all the others combined and without ever batting an eyelash. He also did something else that the gang secretly feared. He did the needle.
