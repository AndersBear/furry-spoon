From my music dictionary, the definition of syncopation (which I'm still trying to understand): syncopation typically takes 3 forms: an accent on a normally unaccented beat or part of a beat; a continuation of a note "over the bar" so as to avoid striking a note on a normally accented beat; the conflict of unequal rhythms in 2 or more parts. You might call it "overlapping." (?)

Now I'm assuming that a lot of music has some syncopation in it. Few songs are just drums, bass, guitar and singer all going "Uh" "Uh" "Uh" at the same time. (?)


Next concept: contrapuntal, from the word "counterpoint." (?) When one musician plays a note that is 3 "steps" higher than a center point, the other musician plays a note that is 3 "steps" lower than the center point. (?)
